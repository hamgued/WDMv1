Welcome to the Coursebook

This coursebook contains the essential materials for the exam. Please check the exam dates on the course page.

The coursebook includes short videos that introduce key topics, along with study packages that provide links to additional resources, primarily readings. Watch the short lecture videos before exploring the materials provided in the study packages.

Be an active student

  • Save the readings as PDF files and take notes as you read. Most browsers allow Print → Save as PDF.
  • You might also consider printing out some of the readings. Reading in print can be more effective than on a screen.

The coursebook is best experienced on a laptop or desktop computer.

Writing Wisdom

Keep some of these nuggets in mind as you work through the course:

"If you don't have time to read, you don't have the time (or the tools) to write."
— Stephen King
"A word after a word after a word is power."
— Margaret Atwood
"To write something you have to risk making a fool of yourself."
— Anne Rice
"You don't start out writing good stuff. You start out writing crap and thinking it's good stuff, and then gradually you get better at it. That's why I say one of the most valuable traits is persistence."
— Octavia E. Butler
"One day I will find the right words, and they will be simple."
— Jack Kerouac
"Easy reading is damn hard writing."
— Nathaniel Hawthorne
"I notice that you use plain, simple language, short words and brief sentences. That is the way to write English — it is the modern way and the best way."
— Mark Twain
"If people cannot write well, they cannot think well, and if they cannot think well, others will do their thinking for them."
— George Orwell
"A skill is something you know how to do. Skill in writing frees you to write what you want to write. Craft enables art."
— Ursula Le Guin
